# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Norwegian upper secondary (VGS) students, ages 15-19. They pull out their phone on the bus, at the kitchen table the night before, or in a study hall. They are anxious about oral exams: unscripted, no notes, one chance. They want to rehearse without a real audience judging them.

## Product Purpose

Karakteren simulates a real Norwegian VGS oral exam: topic draw, follow-up questions, "kan du utdype det?", and a final grade 1-6 with written feedback. It removes the fear of rehearsal by making practice feel low-stakes and slightly funny. Success: student finishes a session and feels more prepared than when they started.

## Brand Personality

Encouraging, dry, secretly on your side. Three words: deadpan, warm, scrappy.

Voice: Blobb the mascot is sarcastic but secretly wants you to succeed. Think Duolingo owl but Norwegian and less earnest. The product itself is direct, never patronising, always in bokmål.

## Anti-references

- Generic edtech blues and greens (Kahoot purple, Google Classroom grey)
- Corporate SaaS dashboards with hero metrics and gradient accents
- Flashcard-app minimalism with nothing alive on screen
- Any design a school IT department would approve without question

## Design Principles

1. **Practice feels safe, not clinical.** Every surface should feel like a scratchpad, not an examination hall.
2. **Blobb carries the personality.** UI stays clean; the mascot absorbs all the wit and warmth.
3. **Grade is a moment, not a decoration.** The 1-6 score appears once, at the right time, with weight. Never as a badge on a card.
4. **Mobile first, always.** The primary use case is a phone. Desktop is a bonus, never the baseline.
5. **Speed over ceremony.** Students open the app 10 minutes before panic sets in. Every flow must reach the exam loop in under 3 taps.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum; aim for AAA on text contrast
- Reduced motion respect (prefers-reduced-motion)
- Large tap targets on all mobile interactions (min 44px)
- Norwegian bokmål primary; nynorsk planned for Phase 6
